Profit / Loss Logic:
Operational Profit / Loss = Expected Sales minus actual expected operating costs. This shows the true accounting operating result.
Owner Profit Gap = Owner Profit Target − Operational Profit/Loss. This shows the missing owner income amount.
Additional Sales Needed = Owner Profit Gap ÷ Contribution Margin. This shows how much additional net sales are needed to close the owner gap.
Expected Cash Flow = Expected Sales + Estimated Tips + Sales Tax. This reflects actual money expected to pass through the restaurant.

Monthly Breakdown

Monthly Breakdown structure:
Costs show what the restaurant must cover. Required Sales show the true operational break-even and the sales needed to also pay the owner target. Expected Reality uses either the seasonality factor or, when selected, the locked Capacity & Revenue monthly target to estimate likely sales. Results show Operational Profit/Loss, Owner Profit Gap, and Additional Sales Needed. Food cost, beverage cost, POS, tips, sales tax, and expected cash flow are based on expected sales. Fixed costs and payroll stay fixed unless an Adjusted Fixed Cost Override is entered.
Variable Cost Correction:
Fixed costs and payroll do not move when expected sales changes. Food cost, beverage cost, POS fees, tips, and sales tax now move with Expected Sales. This means lowering or raising the seasonality factor will correctly change the expected operating costs and the projected profit/loss.
Owner Profit Gap Logic:
Operational Profit / Loss = Expected Sales − Expected Food Cost − Expected Beverage Cost − Expected POS − Adjusted Payroll − Fixed Used.
Owner Profit Gap shows how much owner income is still missing from the monthly target.
Additional Sales Needed converts that missing owner income into the real extra net sales needed, after food, beverage, and POS costs.
Adjusted Fixed Cost Override:
The Monthly Breakdown uses the Adjusted Fixed Cost Override only for the selected months. A value of $0 is valid and will make selected months use $0 fixed cost. This is useful for testing temporary rent reductions, reduced utilities, negotiated expenses, seasonal operations, or any mid-year fixed cost change.
